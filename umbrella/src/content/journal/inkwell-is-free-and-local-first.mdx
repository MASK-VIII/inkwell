# Inkwell is free, local-first writing software — no account, no subscription

[Inkwell](https://inkwell.enterthelimelight.com) is Limelight’s chapter-first writing app for novels and long manuscripts. After a round of product simplification in the public repo, the promise is sharper:

**Draft. Format. Publish — for free, on your machine.**

No account. No subscription. No cloud sync backend. No upsell gated behind a paywall.

## What changed

We removed the billing and cloud-sync surfaces that were pulling the product away from its core job. In practice that means:

- **No Paddle / paid upgrade flow** in the app
- **No Supabase auth or cloud sync** as a product requirement
- **Exports unlocked** — EPUB, print-ready PDF, DOCX, Markdown, and plain text without a “pro” gate
- **Local-only manuscripts** in the browser or Windows desktop app, with portable `.inkwell.zip` backups you control

If you want a writing tool that stays out of your way and does not ask you to rent your own chapters back from a SaaS dashboard, this is the version we meant to ship.

## Who Inkwell is for

Inkwell is built for people who think in **chapters**, not nested folders:

- new authors finishing a first manuscript
- novelists who want a calm spine of chapters instead of a document swamp
- self-publishers who need EPUB and print-ready PDF when the draft is ready

You can start writing without creating an account. Your library stays on the device until you export it.

## Local-first on purpose

“Local-first” here is not a slogan. It is the architecture:

1. Your manuscript lives in local storage (web) or the desktop app.
2. Backups are files you export — not a silent sync you hope is correct.
3. There is no subscription to renew before you can open yesterday’s chapter.

That is a deliberate tradeoff. You lose multi-device cloud sync. You gain ownership, simplicity, and a tool that still works when the network does not.
